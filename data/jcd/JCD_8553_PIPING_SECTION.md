## SECTION 3: ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

### Why Budget Rates Were Too High

1. **Shop Fabrication Quality**
   - Spools arrived with good fit-up - minimal field adjustment
   - This is key advantage of shop-fab vs. field-fab
   - Budget may have assumed more field fitting

2. **Spool Delivery Sequence**
   - Spools delivered in installation sequence (need to verify)
   - No waiting/staging/re-handling

3. **Equipment Availability**
   - Crane availability was good (shared with J&M building)
   - No rigging bottlenecks

4. **Crew Efficiency**
   - Pipefitters were experienced with this scope
   - Good weather conditions (protected inside building)

5. **Estimating Assumptions**
   - Heavy Bid rates may have been legacy industrial rates
   - Did not account for modern shop fabrication quality
   - Over-estimated field weld count (most welds done in shop)

### One Area of Concern: Spec 50 Field Welds (+39%)

- 30 welds budgeted at 5.0 MH/EA
- 208 MH actual = 6.9 MH/EA achieved
- **Question:** Were there added welds? Or more difficult than expected?

## SECTION 6: KEY TAKEAWAYS

### For Future Estimating:

1. **Reduce H/E Pipe rates by 40-50%** when:
   - Using quality shop fabricator
   - Spools delivered in sequence
   - Inside building (weather protected)

2. **Reduce bolt-up rates by 50-70%** for:
   - New construction (not tie-ins)
   - Standard flange ratings
   - Good access/rigging

3. **Keep field weld rates conservative** until:
   - Exact weld count is known
   - Weld types are defined (socket vs butt)
   - Access conditions evaluated

4. **Reduce valve installation rates by 60-70%** for:
   - New construction
   - Good rigging access
   - Standard valve types

### Warning Signs to Watch:

- **Tie-in welds** typically run 2x new construction
- **Confined space** installations add 50-100%
- **Specialty alloys** (duplex, Hastelloy) add 25-50%
- **Field routing changes** can blow up MH quickly

### Lessons Learned - 63" HDPE
