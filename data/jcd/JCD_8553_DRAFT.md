**Key Project Characteristics:**
- **Below-Grade Structure**: The entire pump station sits below existing grade, similar to a basement. The structure is excavated into the tailings impoundment embankment.
- **Massive Mat Slab**: 5'-6" thick mat foundation with heavy reinforcement, approximately 220' x 130' footprint, placed in three major pours rather than the many optional construction joints shown on design drawings.
- **Central Octagon Tank**: The "octagon" (CO_ cost codes) is an octagonal slurry collection tank in the center of the structure with ~30' tall walls, same height as perimeter walls. Tailings slurry is delivered via 63" HDPE pipeline through the inlet structure/tunnel.
- **Loading Dock with Geofoam**: Elevated platform section filled with geofoam to bring it to existing grade level for truck access.
- **Night Pours on Active Tailings Impoundment**: Concrete pours started at midnight due to site logistics. Required dedicated gate guards and escort crews for every concrete truck — a significant unknown cost at bid time.
- **Cold Weather Concrete**: Substantial winter work requiring heated enclosures, blankets, and glycol heaters for cure protection.
