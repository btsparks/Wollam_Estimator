## 1. SCOPE SUMMARY

Structural steel program for the SPD Pump Station including building superstructure, inlet structure steel, stair towers, access platforms, handrails, and bridge crane installation. Scope split between subcontracted fabrication/erection and self-performed installation work.

**Primary Structural Elements:**
- **Steel Building Superstructure**: Pre-engineered metal building (PEMB) covering the pump station, including columns, rafters, purlins, girts, and roofing/siding
- **Inlet Structure Steel**: Stainless steel components for the tailings inlet structure (~15 TON)
- **Stair Towers**: Four pre-fabricated stair towers providing access from grade to the below-grade pump station (~27 TON)
- **Access Platforms**: Elevated platforms throughout the facility for equipment access (~57 TON misc structural)
- **Handrails**: Galvanized and epoxy-coated handrail systems (~1,860 LF)
- **Bridge Crane**: 18.5-ton overhead crane for pump maintenance

**Contracting Strategy:**
- **J&M Steel**: Subcontracted for primary structural steel fabrication and erection
- **Crane Sub**: Subcontracted for bridge crane installation  
- **Wollam Self-Perform**: Stair tower installation, handrail, miscellaneous platforms, inlet steel

**Bid Production:**
- 30 MH/TON = 0.30 TON/crew-hour
- 27 TON ÷ 0.30 TON/hr = 90 crew hours = 9 shifts

**⚠️ CRITICAL VARIANCE — Stair Towers:**

| Metric | Bid Assumption | Actual | Variance |
|--------|----------------|--------|----------|
| Quantity | 27 TON | 27 TON | 0% |
| Total MH | 810 | 1,630 | **+101%** |
| MH/TON | 30.0 | 60.4 | **+101%** |
| Total Cost | $47,224 | $72,451 | +53% |
| $/TON | $1,749 | $2,683 | +53% |

**Root Cause Analysis (PM Feedback):**

The stair tower installation ran **2x budget on manhours**. Primary issues identified:

1. **Fabrication Quality Issues (Alumasteel)**: 
   - Shop fabrication had major problems — handrails didn't line up
   - Significant rework required in the field due to poor fitment
   - Not enough "field fit" pieces were included in the design/fab package

2. **Poor Field Execution & Planning**:
   - Installation crew did not operate efficiently
   - Tracking issues — field changes weren't documented properly
   - Daily/weekly planning deficiencies

3. **Critical Path Conflicts**:
   - Stair tower crew constantly had to accommodate the critical path
   - Concrete pours, pump pad construction always took priority
   - Crew couldn't maintain consistent production rhythm

**Key Lesson:** The overrun was **NOT** primarily due to below-grade access constraints. It was a combination of:
- **Bad fabrication/fitment** (supplier issue)
- **Poor field management** (Wollam execution issue)
- **Schedule conflicts** (project sequencing issue)

**Lessons for Future Estimates:**
1. **Fabrication QC**: Require shop inspection for prefab stair towers before shipment. Verify handrail alignment, tread spacing, and connection points.
2. **Field Fit Allowance**: Include explicit "field fit" pieces in fabrication package (allow 5-10% additional for adjustments).
3. **Dedicated Crew**: Don't share stair tower crew with other critical path work — either fully resource it or plan for extended duration.
4. **Production Rate**: For stair towers with potential fitment issues or secondary priority, use **50-60 MH/TON** (not 30 MH/TON). For well-fabricated towers with dedicated crew, 30-35 MH/TON is achievable.

**⚠️ CRITICAL VARIANCE — Wall Supports (2456):**

Wall-mounted pipe supports went **140% over budget** on MH. Root causes:

1. **Access Constraints**: Wall supports required scaffolding/manlift access in congested areas
2. **Concrete Anchor Installation**: Drilling and setting anchors in concrete walls is more labor-intensive than ground supports
3. **Alignment/Leveling**: Precise alignment requirements for wall-mounted supports
4. **Coordination with Piping**: Supports installed concurrent with pipe, requiring frequent repositioning

**Recommended Rates for Future:**

| Support Type | Budget Rate | Achieved Rate | **Recommended** |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Ground Supports (small) | 3.25 MH/EA | 3.32 MH/EA | **3.50 MH/EA** |
| Ground Supports (large) | 5.00 MH/EA | 6.39 MH/EA | **6.50 MH/EA** |
| Wall Supports | 5.80 MH/EA | 13.94 MH/EA | **12-14 MH/EA** |

**Key Lesson:** Wall-mounted pipe supports require **2-2.5x the labor** of ground supports due to access, anchoring, and alignment requirements. Do NOT use ground support rates for wall supports.

4. **Anchor bolt tolerance** — Coordinate closely with concrete crews on embed placement for steel connections
