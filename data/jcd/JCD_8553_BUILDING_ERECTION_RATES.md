## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This analysis compares Wollam's Heavy Bid self-perform estimate for building erection against J&M Steel Solutions' actual achieved production rates. **This data is critical for future building estimates** — historically, Wollam has struggled to find reliable rates for this work type.

### KEY FINDINGS

| Work Type | Wollam Estimate | J&M Achieved | Variance |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|----------|
| **Primary Steel** | 13.5 MH/TON | **5.4-8.0 MH/TON** | **-40% to -60%** |
| **Secondary Steel** | 30.9 MH/TON | **20.3 MH/TON** | **-34%** |
| **Overall Steel** | 17.4 MH/TON | **19.1 MH/TON** | +10% |
| **Siding/Panels** | (sub by Vesta) | **~0.065 MH/SF** | — |

**Bottom Line:** Wollam's estimate was **conservative on primary steel** but reasonably accurate on secondary. J&M achieved significantly better production on heavy steel erection than Wollam assumed.

## 4. RATE COMPARISON — ESTIMATE vs. ACTUAL

### Primary Steel Erection

| Source | MH/TON | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Wollam Estimate** | 13.5 | SSL 9-man crew |
| **J&M Best Days** | 5.4 | Primary only, focused work |
| **J&M Mixed Days** | 8.0 | Primary + secondary concurrent |
| **J&M Average** | 7.0 | All primary-related days |

**Analysis**: Wollam's estimate was **CONSERVATIVE by 40-60%** on primary steel. J&M consistently achieved better than estimated production, even on mixed days.

**Recommendation**: Use **8-10 MH/TON** for primary steel erection (columns, crane girders, main beams). Use **6 MH/TON** only if dedicated focus on heavy steel with no concurrent secondary work.

### Secondary Steel Erection

| Source | MH/TON | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Wollam Estimate** | 30.9 | SSL 9-man crew |
| **J&M Achieved** | 20.3 | 12-man crew average |

**Analysis**: Wollam's estimate was **CONSERVATIVE by 34%** on secondary steel. J&M achieved significantly better production on girts/purlins.

**Recommendation**: Use **20-25 MH/TON** for secondary steel (girts, purlins, light framing).

### Overall Steel Erection (All-In Rate)

| Source | MH/TON | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Wollam Estimate** | 17.4 | Weighted average of activities |
| **J&M Achieved** | 19.1 | Total project / total tons |

**Analysis**: Overall rate is close to estimate when welding/bolting time is included. The conservatism on erection rates is offset by the significant welding/connection time required for this building (crane girder CJPs, moment connections).

**Recommendation**: Use **18-20 MH/TON** for all-in building erection rate including connections.

### Siding/IMP Installation

- **MH/SF**: 0.065

## 7. LESSONS LEARNED

### What Worked Well
1. **J&M Relationship**: Good subcontractor relationship from previous building project paid off
2. **Material Savings**: Competitive bidding on steel packages saved ~20%
3. **Primary Steel Production**: Achieved 40-60% better than estimated on heavy steel
4. **Crane Coordination**: Using J&M's crane for bridge crane install saved ~$67K

### What We'd Do Differently
1. **Estimate Primary Steel Lower**: 8-10 MH/TON is achievable, not 13.5 MH/TON
2. **Account for Welding Time**: Heavy crane buildings need significant CJP time — budget separately
3. **Track Production by Type**: Daily tracking of primary vs. secondary helps calibrate rates
4. **Consider Self-Perform**: With these rates validated, self-perform may be competitive

### Key Risk Factors
1. **Weather**: Lost 30 of 75 days to weather/delays (40% impact)
2. **Crane Availability**: Multiple days lost waiting for crane
3. **RTK Shutdowns**: Safety stops disrupted production rhythm
4. **CJP Welding**: Crane girder connections took longer than expected
