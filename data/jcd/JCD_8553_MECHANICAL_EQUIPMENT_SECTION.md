**Why Epoxy Grout Takes More Labor:**
1. **Surface Preparation** - Concrete must be clean, dry, and profiled

3. **Mixing** - Precise ratios, limited pot life
4. **Placement** - Must be continuous, no cold joints
5. **Tooling** - Specialized tools, cleanup between pours
6. **Alignment** - Precision leveling during cure
7. **QC** - Inspection holds, testing requirements

**⚠️ MISSED IN BID: Forming & Stripping (F/S) for Epoxy Grout** - See Section 4.

**Recommended Rate:** Use **20-22 MH/CY** for epoxy grout placement ONLY. Add F/S separately.

### 4.5 Grouting Lessons Learned

1. **Epoxy grout is labor-intensive** - Budget 20+ MH/CY, not 14-15. The surface prep, mixing, and placement requirements drive labor.

3. **Cementitious grout rates vary by application:**
   - Steel base plates (production): 3.0-3.5 MH/BAG
   - Precision/small quantity: 5.0+ MH/BAG

4. **Temperature matters** - Epoxy has strict temperature requirements. Winter work requires heating/enclosures.

5. **Pot life is critical** - Epoxy grout has limited working time. Plan pours carefully to avoid waste.

## 7. LESSONS LEARNED

### What Went Over Budget

1. **Epoxy Grout F/S was MISSED entirely** - The bid included grout placement (14.4 MH/CY) but did NOT include forming and stripping. Epoxy grout requires tight, sealed forms - this added 721 MH (~30 MH/CY) that wasn't budgeted.

2. **Epoxy Grout Placement (+41%)** - Even without F/S, placement ran over due to surface prep, temperature control, and precision requirements.

3. **HPU Sheds (+116%)** - Assembly complexity underestimated. Includes structural, equipment mounting, and weatherproofing.

4. **Pump Rework (Extra Work)** - Pumps had to be removed and reset **2 times** because design was off and pump base concrete required modifications. This was 584 MH of extra work that should have been avoided with better coordination.

5. **Pump Assembly (CO)** - Large pumps arrived in **more pieces than expected** at bid time. Required 356 MH of additional field assembly labor approved as a Change Order.

### What Went Under Budget

1. **Sump Pumps (-56%)** - Smaller, simpler installations. Budget was conservative.

2. **Large AHU (-92%)** - Work was done by HVAC sub. Only rigging assist tracked to Wollam.

3. **SW Tank (-81%)** - Simple tank set. Budget was too high.

### Key Recommendations

1. **Epoxy Grout Program - COMPLETE ESTIMATE:**
   - **Placement:** 20-22 MH/CY
   - **F/S (Forming & Stripping):** 28-32 MH/CY
   - **TOTAL LABOR:** 50 MH/CY
   - **Material:** ~$7,800/CY (For-Shor or equivalent)
   - **ALL-IN:** ~$10,000/CY
   - **Winter work:** Add 10-15% contingency for tenting, heating, and weather delays
   - Include contingency for rework
   - **Clarify internal cavity fill requirement** - RTK's CO to fill internal pump base cavities was unexpected. Ask this question during bid.

2. **Pump Installation - Verify Before Concrete:**
   - Get final pump vendor drawings BEFORE pump pad concrete pour
   - Verify base plate dimensions, anchor bolt locations, and grout pocket depth
   - Coordinate with vendor on delivery condition (assembled vs disassembled)
   - Budget assembly MH if pumps ship as kit-of-parts

3. **Pump Set/Reset Contingency:**
   - Large pump set: 140-150 MH/EA
   - If design uncertainty exists, budget for potential reset: add 100 MH/EA contingency
   - Removal labor if needed: ~40-50 MH/EA

4. **HPU/Equipment Sheds:**
   - Do not underestimate assembly time
   - Budget 80-90 MH/EA including equipment mounting

### Estimating Misses to Avoid

| Miss | Impact | How to Avoid |
|------|--------|--------------|
| No F/S for epoxy grout | 721 MH unbudgeted | Always include F/S at 30 MH/CY |
| Pump assembly (pieces) | 356 MH CO | Confirm delivery condition with vendor |
| Pump base design issues | 584 MH rework | Verify dimensions before concrete pour |
| Internal cavity grout fill | 2nd lift CO | Clarify grout scope with owner/engineer |
