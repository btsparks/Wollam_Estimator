# KEY LESSONS LEARNED

## Project-Wide

1. **2:1 Slope VE** saved ~$2.4M in earthwork - always request geotech analysis early for tailings work
2. **CPI of 1.37** indicates conservative estimating - validate historical rates
3. **Mine site training** was 380% over budget - budget 2-3x for RTK/mine work
4. **Owner-provided safety** eliminated need for dedicated safety staff

## By Discipline

### Concrete
- Wall F/S at **0.28 MH/SF is gold standard** - validated by this project
- **3-pour strategy** for mat foundation saved 57% on pumping costs
- **Equipment pad embeds** add 40-50% to F/S labor - account for anchor bolts, sleeves

### Structural Steel
- **Pipe supports at 12 MH/EA** (8 fab + 4 install) - track separately from building steel
- **J&M at $3,766/TON** is competitive for building erection only
- **Epoxy grout at 50 MH/CY all-in** - include surface prep, mixing, placement, finishing

### Piping
- **Flanged connections at 7 MH/joint** for 20-28" RLS/CS
- **63" HDPE fusing at 0.85 MH/LF** - specialized equipment required
- **Hydrotesting at 50 MH/test** - include prep, pressurization, documentation

### Earthwork
- **Full-time floor dozer essential** for truck production - "keeps trucks running smooth"
- **4 trucks optimal** for short haul (500'-1,500'), 2 sufficient for very short (<500')
- **Wheel-rolling compaction** achieved spec for whole tailings without separate roller

### Electrical
- **$136/SF for heavy industrial** electrical - confirmed benchmark
- **Self-perform support at $10/LF** for duct bank EX/BF

### General Conditions
- **Survey at $18K/month** during active earthwork/concrete phases
- **QC testing at 0.65%** of job cost when contractor-provided
- **GL Insurance at 0.72%** - consistent benchmark
